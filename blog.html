Hurricane Michael made landfall over Florida Panhandle

Figure: Animation of the hurricane Michael (1-hour intervel from 7 - 10 Oct, 2018) using the 10-km resolution IMERG data.

Hurricane Michael made catastrophic landfall over Florida Panhandle (near Mexico Beach, about 20 miles from Panama City) on October 10, 2018, with winds up to 155 mph. Michael made landfall as a Category 4 on the Saffir-Simpson Hurricane Wind Scale. It was also the most powerful storm to hit the US mainland since Hurricane Andrew in 1992, causing over $25 billion in damage and 16 fatalities. 16 tornadoes associated with Michael have been reported in Florida (2), Georgia (3), South Carolina (4) and Virginia(7), however they only caused minimal damages.
